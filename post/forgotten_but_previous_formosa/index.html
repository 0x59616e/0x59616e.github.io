Forgotten But Previous Formosa

August 28, 2025

He lost.

From the pitcher’s mound to the catcher’s mitt, 18.44 meters apart, with one strike to go for the win, only to see a base hit tie the game at 3-3.

He struggled, he faltered, he yielded.

The cherry blossoms fell around him as he walked off the mound, each one carrying a letter of his name.

It was the name that could define him and connect him.

He lost the game, and he forgot his name.

But he never lost his faith.

Even though everybody lost faith in him, he never lost his faith.

Day after day. He waited, he endured, he prepared. Year after year, he tumbled, he stood up, he rose up.

He rose up from the dark valley. He rose up to the sunlit mound, the mound where he lost the game eleven years ago, the mound where he forgot his name eleven years ago.

He stumbled, he stroked, he survived.

He survived to the bottom of the 9th, 2 out, runner on first, he was ahead 4-0, and there came the full count offering.

The batter hit.

The ball stormed toward first base, tore through his mind, unearthed all the memories he buried eleven years ago.

The memories rose up, formed into dark clouds, spread over the sky, overwhelmed him.

But there were stars rising up from the stands, each one carrying a letter of his name.

They were the stars that accompanied him when he struggled.

They were the stars that guided him when he lost his way.

The stars shone out a path guiding the ball into the glove of the first baseman. 3 out. Game over.

The air was frozen, the cheer thundered through, and the eternity was earned.

His name echoed around.

His name is Team Taiwan.

We are Team Taiwan

Let’s go to the stadium together, cheering together, winning together, and even losing together. Let the name ring around the field, knowing what defines us, connects us, and get one fact straight—we will get better and better.

Go forward with faith. Go forward with our name.

We are Team Taiwan.
